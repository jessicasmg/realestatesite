What is Section 8?

The Housing Choice Voucher Program, commonly known as Section 8, is a federal program that helps low-income families, elderly persons, veterans, and individuals with disabilities afford safe, quality housing in the private rental market.

The program is funded by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) and administered locally by the Aurora Housing Authority.

How Rent Payment Works

Your Portion

You typically pay 30-40% of your adjusted monthly income toward rent and utilities.

Housing Authority's Portion

The Aurora Housing Authority pays the remaining portion directly to your landlord each month.

This partnership between you, the housing authority, and your landlord ensures that rent is paid reliably and on time.

Applying for Section 8 in Aurora

To apply for a Section 8 voucher in Aurora, you'll need to work with the Aurora Housing Authority. Their waiting list opens and closes periodically based on available funding.

Checking Waiting List Status

The Section 8 waiting list opens and closes periodically. When open, applications must be submitted online through the MyHousing Applicant Portal. Check the Aurora Housing Authority website for current status.

Get Notified: Sign up at aurorahousing.org to receive email notifications when the waiting list opens. Announcements are also published in the Aurora Sentinel newspaper.

Eligibility Requirements

Eligibility is based on your total annual gross income and family size:

  • Must be a U.S. citizen or have eligible immigration status
  • Income must not exceed 50% of area median income (AMI)
  • 75% of vouchers go to applicants earning below 30% of AMI
  • Must be 18 years of age or older

Special Voucher Programs

Aurora Housing Authority offers several specialized voucher programs in addition to standard Section 8:

VASH Program

Veterans Affairs Supportive Housing combines rental assistance with case management services for homeless veterans. Referrals come through the VA.

Emergency Housing Vouchers

Assists individuals fleeing domestic violence or experiencing homelessness. Referrals through Gateway Domestic Violence Services or Metro Denver Homeless Initiative.

Mainstream Vouchers

For non-elderly persons with disabilities. Additional preference given to those receiving services from partner agencies like Aurora Mental Health.

Family Unification Program

Helps families where lack of adequate housing is a primary factor in child placement. Referrals through Arapahoe County Human Services.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I move to a new apartment and keep my Section 8?

Yes! You must be in good standing with your landlord and have lived in your unit for at least one year. Provide 30-days notice to your landlord, and your housing coordinator will issue you a new voucher giving you 60 days to find a new unit.

What if my income changes?

Contact the Aurora Housing Authority immediately. If your income decreases, your rental portion will decrease and the housing authority's contribution will increase. If your income increases, your portion may go up. The program is designed to adjust to your financial situation.

Does the housing authority pay security deposits?

No, the Colorado Division of Housing does not pay security deposits. You'll need to arrange the security deposit directly with your landlord.

What happens if I disagree with a housing authority decision?

All Section 8 applicants, participants, and property owners are entitled to an informal appeal and review process. You have the right to challenge decisions that affect your participation in the program.

Can I use my voucher to rent from SMG Property?

Absolutely! We welcome Section 8 voucher holders and have extensive experience working with the Aurora Housing Authority. Contact us to learn about available units and schedule a showing.
