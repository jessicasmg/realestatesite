A Community for Everyone

One of Aurora's greatest strengths is its diversity

About 1 in 5 residents were born outside the United States, and the city is home to thriving communities from more than 130 countries, with over 160 languages spoken in schools. Aurora is recognized as a majority-minority city where different backgrounds are celebrated.

This rich cultural mix is reflected in everything from the more than 250 ethnic eateries representing cuisines from around the world to community events and neighborhood life. The city's cultural scene includes venues like the Aurora Fox Arts Center and the Vintage Theatre offering live performances year-round.

For newcomers to the United States or anyone looking for a place where diversity is a strength, Aurora offers a genuine sense of belonging.

Affordable Living Near Denver

Aurora provides a more affordable alternative to Denver while keeping you close to everything the metro area has to offer. Rental prices in Aurora consistently run lower than in Denver and the surrounding metro area, making it an appealing choice for people who want access to urban amenities without the premium price tag.

Whether you're working in Denver, Aurora, or elsewhere in the metro area, Aurora's central location makes commuting manageable. The city is easily accessible via I-225 and the RTD light rail system, with proximity to Denver International Airport and downtown Denver attractions.

Aurora is home to some of the highest-rated schools in Colorado, making it an excellent choice for families. Aurora Quest K-8 is the top-rated middle school and second-ranked elementary school in Colorado, while Grandview High School is ranked 22nd among all high schools in the state.

Aurora Public Schools is the fifth-largest public school system in Colorado, serving over 40,000 students as one of the most diverse school districts in the state. The public school system is committed to supporting both students and teachers. Aurora teachers received the highest pay raise in the Denver metro area in 2022, reflecting the district's investment in quality education.

Stay Active Year-Round

Living in Aurora means you're close to the Colorado lifestyle everyone talks about. The mountains are just a short drive away, offering hiking and mountain biking in the warmer months and skiing and snowboarding in the winter.

Within the city itself, Aurora manages over 100 parks and more than 5,000 acres of open space. Cherry Creek State Park and the Aurora Reservoir offer miles of trails, picnic areas, boating, and paddleboarding opportunities.

Residents can also enjoy six public golf courses, hiking and biking trails, horseback riding, and camping throughout the area. Within Aurora, you'll find rock climbing gyms, cycling facilities, and recreational programs for all ages and skill levels.

A Growing Economy

Aurora's economy is strong and diverse, with a 3.3% unemployment rate significantly below the 3.7% national average. The city continues to grow, bringing new businesses and jobs across healthcare, education, retail, technology, aerospace, and defense sectors.
